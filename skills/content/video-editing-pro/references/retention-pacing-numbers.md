## Hook retention benchmarks

| Retention at 3s | Diagnosis | Action |
|---|---|---|
| >70% | Strong hook | Optimize rest of video |
| 50–70% | Partial hook | Faster text delivery, stronger first word, more visual motion |
| <50% | Hook failing | Re-cut or re-shoot the opening |

Source: go-viral.app — these are treated as platform-derived benchmarks, not theoretical.

## Pacing: visual change frequency

| Change interval | Effect |
|---|---|
| < 1.2s | Registers as noise, brain tunes out |
| 1.5–2s | Optimal for Shorts/Reels (2026 target) |
| 2–2.5s | Acceptable, slight attention fade |
| > 2.5s | Retention dip |
| > 8s | Significant viewer drop |

Source: Aibrify: "The 2026 pacing target is a visual change roughly every 1.5–2 seconds."

"Visual change" includes: cut, camera move, text overlay appearing, zoom/crop change, color shift. In a talking-head without B-roll, a new caption line or text overlay counts.

## Cut frequency by energy phase (VlogLikePro energy management system)

Building to a reveal:
- 30s before reveal: cut every 4s
- 20s before reveal: cut every 3s
- 10s before reveal: cut every 2s
- Reveal moment: hard cut to silence/stillness, hold 0.5–1s

Macro energy curve:
- Intro: HIGH
- Setup: MEDIUM
- Main content: MEDIUM-HIGH
- Climax: HIGHEST
- Outro: MEDIUM

## Hook composition rules

- Hook must land within 1.5–3 seconds
- 3+ cuts in the first 3 seconds boost watch time by 58% (autoshorts.app, citing neuroscience of "change signals")
- Optimal cut timing in hook window: 0.1–0.3s cuts for dynamism (postigniter.com)
- Eliminate any logo, name card, or "hey guys" line in first 2–3 seconds — "Expect a 10–20 percentage-point gain in average view duration from this single change" (Aibrify)

## Retention curve shapes

**Cliff** (failure):
- 30–50% drop in first 3 seconds
- Cause: weak or missing hook

**Hump** (good):
- Sustains above 60% mid-video
- High share rates
- Sign of strong mid-video content

**Plateau** (algorithm favorite):
- Flat above 70% end-to-end
- Spike above 100% on second 1 (rewatches)
- How to achieve: "tightly paced visual content where no single moment fails retention and the final frame loops cleanly into the first"

## Micro-hook cadence (VlogLikePro)

"Something interesting should happen every 10–15 seconds, especially in the first 2 minutes."

This is the micro-pattern-interrupt rule. Each 10–15s block needs one of: new information, a surprising claim, a visual escalation, or a setup for the next beat.

---

## Speed ramping values (VlogLikePro)

- Fast sections: 105–115% speed (barely perceptible, creates energy)
- Normal sections: 100%
- Slow motion: 30–60%

These are for energy management, not stylistic slow-mo.

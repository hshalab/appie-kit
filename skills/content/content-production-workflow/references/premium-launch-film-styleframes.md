# Premium launch film styleframe gate

Session learning from Instant Appie v4.5 Direction B work.

## Trigger
Use this when creating high-fidelity launch films, carousel-to-video concepts, SaaS product motion, or branded short-form video assets where the user expects premium design quality.

## Durable workflow lesson
Do not jump from brief directly to full rendered video. For premium motion work, create an approval gate first:

1. Choose one art direction and one visual metaphor.
2. Produce 3 polished static styleframes only:
   - opener
   - product proof
   - CTA or end card
3. QA the styleframes visually before delivery.
4. Only after approval, build storyboard, animatic, then final animation.

## User correction captured
When the output felt generic or cheap, the user rejected it strongly. The fix was not more rendering. The fix was to stop, research premium references, pick a disciplined direction, and create styleframes first.

## Anti-slop rules for this class
- Avoid generic AI dashboard grids, random glow cards, and decoration-heavy cyber backgrounds.
- Do not paste a mascot on top of serious proof frames. If used, integrate it with lighting, scale, staging, shadow and purpose.
- Real product/PDF proof should be redesigned into a readable product surface, not pasted as tiny screenshots.
- For mobile-first vertical assets, proof modules must be large enough to read at phone size.
- If proof cards feel cluttered, reduce the count. In the Appie v4.5 pass, 6 tiny tiles became 4 large modules and the quality improved.
- Use one core material or metaphor, such as live OS layer, liquid glass, command interface or product canvas. Do not mix multiple visual metaphors.

## Direction B pattern that worked
For a serious AI-business guide, the strongest direction was: "the PDF becomes a live operating system."

Visual grammar:
- dark premium surface
- restrained gold/cyan accents
- large typography
- few proof modules
- real source material cropped as supporting evidence
- mascot as subtle guide presence only

## QA checklist before delivery
- Can the headline be read in a contact sheet and on a phone?
- Is there one clear metaphor?
- Does every visual element support the proof or CTA?
- Is the mascot integrated rather than pasted?
- Are there fewer, stronger modules instead of many small cards?
- Did we deliver styleframes for approval before promising a full movie?

# Weblyfe blog audit (2026)

Session-specific findings from reviewing the live Weblyfe blog archive.

## Current archive snapshot
- 8 posts visible on `/blog-weblyfe`
- Main themes:
  - AI / automation
  - Webflow vs WordPress decision content
  - story-driven design and positioning
  - authority / lecture / founder proof
  - local Dutch lead-gen
- Most posts are roughly 500 to 1,300 words and use clean H1/H2 structure.

## What is working
- Strong founder voice and real examples
- Good mix of thought leadership and proof
- Clear relevance to Weblyfe positioning around design + automation + Webflow
- `Webflow vs WordPress 2026` is the strongest high-intent commercial post in the current archive

## Gaps
- The archive is more standalone than cluster-driven
- English and Dutch content are mixed without an explicit content strategy split
- Some posts are brand statements rather than search-intent answers
- Internal-linking looks present but not yet organized as a deliberate authority network

## Practical interpretation
- Treat the blog as three main clusters:
  1. AI workflows for service businesses
  2. Webflow / platform decision content
  3. story-driven / conversion-focused web design
- Use supporting posts to link into a pillar page and conversion pages like `/projects`, `/proposal`, `/university`, and `/links`
- For the active publishing cadence, 2 posts per week is a good fit if each post is tied to a cluster and a primary keyword

## Publishing heuristic
- Keep the highest-intent comparison post near the top of each cluster.
- Pair each proof or story post with one search-driven support post.
- Avoid publishing isolated one-off topics unless they reinforce a cluster or conversion goal.

CORE GOAL
"Your goal here is to make the best YOUTUBE videos possible. That's the number one goal of this production company."
Not the most technically proficient. Not the funniest. Not the highest production quality. Algorithmically optimized for YouTube.

THREE CRITICAL METRICS
1. CTR (Click-Through Rate) — set by thumbnail and title
2. AVD (Average View Duration) — set by structure and pacing
3. AVP (Average View Percentage) — set by length and engagement

THUMBNAILS AND TITLES
- "The title and thumbnail on the videos you will be producing set the expectations for the viewer."
- Mismatch between thumbnail promise and video delivery = immediate viewer dropout.
- Extremity wins: "I Survived" beats "I Spent". "Bananas Are The Worst Food On Earth" beats "I Don't Like Bananas".
- "I Spent 50 Hours In Ketchup" outperforms "I Spent 50 Hours In My Front Yard".
- All production teams must know the exact title and thumbnail BEFORE filming begins.

MINUTE-BY-MINUTE STRUCTURE (long-form, 13:37 avg)
Minutes 0-1: "This is why we freak out so much about the first minute and go so above and beyond to make it the best we freakin can."
  - Establish hype
  - Match thumbnail promise immediately
  - Pre-plan opening dialogue
  - Strong lighting at video start correlates with reduced viewer dropout
  - Losing ~21 million viewers from 60 million initial clicks = acceptable result

Minutes 1-3: Transition from hype to execution
  - Use "crazy progression" — cover multiple story beats quickly
  - Make the viewer "super invested" in what happens next

3-Minute Mark: First "re-engagement"
  - Spectacular content unique to MrBeast
  - Example: crane lifting a house 30 seconds into a video (no narrative purpose, pure wow-factor)
  - "Anything that no other YouTuber can do"

Minutes 3-6: Most exciting, simple, stimulating content
  - Quick scene changes
  - Make viewer "fall in love" with the story/characters
  - Simple enough for 50 million simultaneous viewers to understand

6-Minute Mark: Second re-engagement
  - Higher production spectacular
  - Advances narrative momentum

Back Half: Long explanations acceptable here; unexpected failures become content

ENDINGS
"Video endings must always be abrupt to protect retention."
Signal conclusion only for prize/payoff buildup. Never telegraph the end early.

THE "WOW FACTOR"
Beyond measurable metrics: "Anything that no other YouTuber can do."
Not tracked in data but invaluable for brand differentiation.
"Who the fuck else on YouTube can do that lol" — the benchmark question.

SCRIPTING RULES
- Simple, universal messaging for 50+ million simultaneous viewers
- "Good content can be anything" — infinite possibilities with simple objects
- Don't fake scenarios; authenticity is recognized by audiences
- Integrate brand deals as entertainment, not ad reads
- Pre-write multiple backup content pieces (if one idea has 50/50 success odds, write another)
- "Creativity saves money": year's supply of Doritos (1,825 packs at $1 each = $1,825) beats $20,000 cash for same impact

STAIR-STEPPING FORMAT
Progressive escalation: $1 firework → $100,000 firework. Stakes increase = sustained engagement.

LAST-TO-LEAVE FORMAT
Unknown winner until the end = natural retention mechanism without needing to engineer suspense explicitly.

NO DULL MOMENTS
"You can't fake intensity in videos." Every edit and pacing decision must eliminate low-energy segments.

FINANCIAL DISCIPLINE
"I want money spent to be shown on camera ideally. If you're spending over $10,000 on something and it won't be shown on camera, seriously think about it."

TEAM PHILOSOPHY
- A-Players: "Obsessive, learn from mistakes, coachable, intelligent, don't make excuses, believe in YouTube"
- C-Players: "Poisonous" — transition immediately
- "The amount of hours you work is irrelevant" — results-only
- "Every veteran here has cost me a million dollars... you have nothing to be afraid of" — mistakes are expected

INFORMATION DIET
- "You can't get inspired by things you don't know exist."
- Creative staff must consume cartoons, memes, and culture obsessively
- Monitor trending content across YouTube, TikTok, social media continuously
